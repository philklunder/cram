# Claude Pixel Mascot

## Decisions

- **The mascot's sprites are build output, not source.** `web/public/claude-mascot/{light,dark}/*.png`
  is generated by `web/scripts/build-mascot-sprites.py` from three owner-supplied art packs.
  Never hand-edit the PNGs; change the script and rerun it.
- **The supplied art is normalised, not used as-is.** Every frame is de-rotated, scaled so the
  head matches a canonical width, then pinned by head-centre-x and feet-baseline onto one
  shared 360px canvas whose size is derived from the union of all frames.
- **Speech-bubble text is HTML, never a sprite.** The packs' bubble frames (`brand-*`, `hey-*`,
  `tour-*`, `letsgo-*`, `reading`, `done`) are excluded from the component.
- **Dark variants are generated for bubble frames only** (34 of 68). Wordless poses are
  transparent PNGs that read on both themes; `src()` falls back to `light/` when no `dark/`
  file exists.
- **`theme` is a prop, not inferred.** `"auto"` follows the `dark` class on `<html>`; the
  landing page passes `theme="light"` explicitly.
- **He disengages from the cursor when it stops moving** (5s), amuses himself with weighted
  random idle bits, and sleeps after 30s. Sleep is also the dark-mode resting state.
- **He does not track the cursor during generation.** `mood="reading"` plays the seated
  laptop loop; `done`/`error` are transient reactions that expire back to tracking.
- **Rejected: the `KebeliSamet0/clawd` sprites.** Code-only reuse (animation timings).

## Reasoning

- **Why a build script.** The three packs disagree with each other in ways that produce visible
  bugs when the PNGs are dropped in directly, and every one was found by measuring rather than
  looking:
  - Different canvases (1254px vs 512px) and different head sizes within each.
  - Pack 1 ships **two poses**: the eight directional frames plus `happy`/`sad` tuck their arms
    in *and are rendered at +1.34°*, while `look-center`/`surprised` spread their arms and sit
    square. 1.34° is nothing on smooth art and glaring on blocky pixels — the head band slants
    and the eyes land at different heights.
  - Pack 1 stores fully-transparent pixels as **white** `(254,254,254,0)`, so any non-premultiplied
    resample bleeds a white halo around the character.
  - The "generating" pack is **fully opaque**: its transparency checkerboard is painted pixels,
    alternating `(254,254,254)` / `(248,248,248)`.
  - `glowing-eyes/` has `alpha=255` everywhere (a solid white background) and cyan eyes.
  - `branding/powered-by-claude-confetti.png` and `completion/done-confetti-*.png` are
    byte-identical; `idle-knees` is 16 files but only 5 distinct images.

- **Why HTML for the bubble.** Measured on the normalised sprite, a capital letter in the baked
  bubbles is **3px tall out of 360**. At an 84px mascot it renders **1.2px**; reaching a legible
  7px would need a **471px** mascot. This is a resolution limit, so no dark variant, filter, or
  re-export could fix it. HTML text is crisp at any size, theme-aware, translatable, and reachable
  by a screen reader. The same reasoning excludes the `Done!` frame from the generating pack.

- **Why dark variants only for bubbles.** His eyes are the same black as the bubble text, so a
  naive invert whitens his eyes. The script classifies black pixels by neighbourhood: black
  adjacent to bubble-white is ink, black sitting on the orange body is an eye or a mouth. The
  work frames are excluded entirely — their white rectangles are the paper cards he produces,
  not bubbles, and recolouring them would blacken his output on a dark UI.

- **Why `theme` is explicit.** `landing.module.css` has **zero** dark-mode rules (it's a verbatim
  port of the owner's light-only comp — see `landing-page.md`), while `app/layout.tsx` stamps
  `.dark` on `<html>` from the OS preference. Under `theme="auto"` any visitor with a dark OS
  would have found the mascot asleep on a white page.

- **Why disengagement.** Holding the last directional sprite at a stationary cursor reads as a
  frozen stare, not attention. Idle bits are spaced 3.2–5.8s apart and never repeat back-to-back
  because a constantly fidgeting mascot pulls the eye off the page.

- **Why "sleep on dark mode" was implemented as *starts asleep, wakes on approach*.** Dark mode
  is not night — many users run it permanently — so a literal reading would have disabled cursor
  tracking, the feature's whole point, for a large share of users. `WAKE_RADIUS = 0` restores the
  literal behaviour.

- **Why the clawd art was rejected.** `assets/LICENSE-clawd` carves the artwork out of the repo's
  MIT licence: *"All Rights Reserved … may not be used for commercial purposes … You may NOT
  copy, modify, distribute, or use any artwork from this directory without explicit written
  permission."* It also states the Clawd character is Anthropic's property, so the repo owner
  could not have MIT-licensed it regardless. Cram has a paid tier. Independently, it is a
  *different character* (`#DE886D`, 1×2 rectangular eyes, ground shadow) and would have broken
  the same-character requirement. The **code** is genuinely MIT and its animation timings
  (3.2s breathe, 5s sleep-breathe, `scaleY(0.1)` blink) are reused with attribution in-file.

- **Why translate-only sleep breathing.** A fractional `scale()` on an
  `image-rendering: pixelated` bitmap drops pixel rows unevenly and visibly shears him. The
  breathing runs the entire time he sleeps, so it must translate. The hop and click squash may
  scale — they're over in under a second.

## Implications

- **Changing the art means rerunning the script**, with all three pack paths:
  `python scripts/build-mascot-sprites.py --pack1 <dir> --pack2 <dir> --gen <dir>`.
  The packs live outside the repo (owner's `~/Downloads`), so the generated PNGs are committed
  as the source of truth for the build. `manifest.json` records `canvas`, `bodyWidth`,
  `bodyFraction` and `anchor`; `ClaudeMascot.tsx` mirrors those as constants.
- **`size` is his body width, not the box width.** The box is `size / BODY_FRACTION` because the
  canvas carries speech-bubble headroom. `trim` collapses that headroom for use inside a card.
- **Callers on light-only surfaces must pass `theme="light"`.** Any new dark-incapable surface
  inherits the asleep-on-white bug otherwise.
- **He anchors to `.heroStage`, not `.dashboardShell`.** The shell is `overflow: hidden` and
  3D-rotated, so a child would be clipped at the corner and skewed. `.heroStage` was introduced
  to carry the mockup's width; the `min(100%, 720px)` breakpoint override moved with it.
- **~3.1 MB / 102 PNGs** ship in `public/`. Only the frames a caller can reach are preloaded —
  the 9 work frames load only when the caller passes a `mood` prop.
- Adding a pose means adding it to `plan()` **and** to the preload list, or the first frame pops.

## Open questions

- The 33 bubble-sprite frames and the `reading`/`done` confetti frames are generated but unused.
  Either delete them from `plan()` or find a surface large enough for their baked text.
- The `done` / `error` reactions in `UploadWork` are verified by construction, not observed
  against a live backend. A real PDF has not been pushed through the full arc.
- `surprised` retains a +0.47° residual tilt (under the 0.3° de-rotation threshold at source).
- The mascot is web-only. iOS has no equivalent and no plan for one.
